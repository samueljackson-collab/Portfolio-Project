## Tips for Customization

### Before Sending:
- [ ] Replace all [brackets] with specific information
- [ ] Research the company: mission, recent news, tech stack
- [ ] Identify 2-3 specific projects/achievements from your portfolio that match job requirements
- [ ] Quantify achievements wherever possible (percentages, time saved, uptime achieved)
- [ ] Keep total length to 1 page (350-500 words)
- [ ] Proofread for typos and grammar
- [ ] Match the tone to company culture (formal for enterprise, casual for startups)

### Red Flags to Avoid:
- ❌ Generic cover letter clearly mass-sent
- ❌ Focusing on what you want rather than what you offer
- ❌ Repeating resume word-for-word
- ❌ No specific examples or metrics
- ❌ Spelling company name incorrectly
- ❌ Being too humble ("I think I might be able to..." → "I am confident I can...")

### What Makes a Strong Cover Letter:
- ✅ Shows you researched the company
- ✅ Connects your experience directly to job requirements
- ✅ Includes specific metrics and achievements
- ✅ Demonstrates genuine enthusiasm for the role
- ✅ Tells a story (problem → solution → result)
- ✅ Professional yet personable tone
- ✅ Clear call to action (request for interview)
